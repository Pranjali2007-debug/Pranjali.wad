SAMSUNG

Samsung mobile phones are a diverse range of Android-based devices known collectively as the Samsung Galaxy series, which includes everything from high-end foldables to budget-friendly models. They are characterized by innovative technology, vibrant displays, and custom user interfaces.

♦Key Features and Technology

Operating System:
All current Samsung smartphones run on Google's Android operating system, customized with Samsung's own user interface called One UI.
Displays:
Samsung is well-known for its high-quality displays, primarily using Super AMOLED and Dynamic AMOLED 2X technology, which offers vibrant colors and deep blacks. Many models feature high refresh rates (up to 120Hz) for smooth scrolling and gaming.
Cameras:
Samsung phones often feature advanced multi-lens camera systems, with high-resolution sensors (up to 200MP on flagship models like the Galaxy S24 Ultra) and AI-powered features like Nightography and advanced zoom capabilities.
Performance:
Devices are powered by a range of processors, including Samsung's own Exynos chips and Qualcomm's Snapdragon processors (often "for Galaxy" versions), ensuring powerful performance across different market segments.
Durability and Design:
Many models feature premium materials like Armor Aluminum frames and Gorilla Glass Victus protection, with high IP ratings for water and dust resistance.
Ecosystem:
Samsung has a wide ecosystem of connected devices, including tablets, smartwatches (Galaxy Watch series), and earbuds (Galaxy Buds series), that seamlessly sync with their phones. Product Series Overview Samsung categorizes its phones into several distinct series to cater to different user needs and price points.
